SOLAR PROJECT PERMIT ANALYSIS
==================================================

Project Address: 123 Main St, San Jose, Santa Clara County, CA 95110
System Size: 8.5 MWac

ANALYSIS:
--------------------
For an 8.5 MWac solar project at 123 Main St, San Jose, Santa Clara County, CA 95110, you will need the following permits and approvals:

1. Conditional Use Permit (Santa Clara County)
   - Cost: $10,000–$30,000
   - Timeline: 6–12 months

2. FAA Permit (Form 7460-1, if near airports)
   - Cost: $0
   - Timeline: 1–2 months

3. Nationwide Permit (Section 404, US Army Corps of Engineers, if wetlands impacted)
   - Cost: $5,000–$20,000
   - Timeline: 6–12 months

4. Wetlands Permit (Section 401, USACE/EPA, if applicable)
   - Cost: $5,000–$20,000
   - Timeline: 6–12 months

5. Wildlife Permit (US Fish & Wildlife, if sensitive species present)
   - Cost: $5,000–$15,000
   - Timeline: 6–12 months

6. CEQA Environmental Review (California Environmental Quality Act)
   - Cost: $20,000–$100,000+
   - Timeline: 12–18 months

7. Building/Electrical Permits (City of San Jose)
   - Cost: $10,000–$30,000
   - Timeline: 3–6 months

8. Interconnection Studies and Agreement (PG&E)
   - Timeline: 12–24 months (can run in parallel with other permitting)

Estimated Overall Timeline:
- Permitting (including CEQA): 12–18 months
- Interconnection: 12–24 months (parallel)
- Total development timeline (including site control, diligence, engineering, and construction): 24–36 months

Recommendations:
- Begin environmental and interconnection studies as early as possible, as these are the most time-consuming.
- Engage with Santa Clara County and City of San Jose planning departments early to clarify zoning and permitting requirements.
- Conduct a detailed site and title review to confirm land use compatibility.
- Review environmental constraints (wetlands, species, floodplains) to determine if federal permits are needed.
- Prepare a decommissioning plan as required by county ordinance.

All costs and timelines are estimates and should be confirmed with the relevant agencies. Early diligence and stakeholder engagement are critical to avoid delays.
